# Warm Outbound ICP

**Purpose**

This document defines *who* we are targeting for warm outreach, and the practical filters the team will use to build weekly prospect lists. It is designed to be unambiguous and easy to apply.

---

A short list of market segments where Alex’s experience is most relevant and where senior operators are actively making decisions on monetisation, operating model, product, and partnerships.

| Segment | Who they are | Typical remit |
| --- | --- | --- |

  • Improve KPI clarity, operating rhythm, and delivery throughput across leadership teams-

## Qualification filters

The minimum criteria used to decide whether a company belongs in the outreach environment. These filters reduce noise and keep lists aligned to accounts where there is sufficient scale and operational complexity.

| Filter | Target | Rationale |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Revenue scale** | **$5M–$250M** annual revenue | Indicates operating complexity and budget without being too enterprise-bureaucratic or competing with MBB sized firms. |
| **Operating context** | At least one applies: **flat/slowing growth**, **scaling with structural strain**, **post-acquisition / integration**, **building a new monetisation line** | These are situations where execution, operating model, and revenue performance are under active review |
| **Geography** | **US primary**, **UK/EU secondary** | Highest concentration of relevant operators, partners, and comparable market dynamics |

## Typical Target Personas

### Persona 1: The Media CEO / GM (P&L Owner)

**Who**

CEO, President, or GM running a publisher, media division, network, or monetisation unit.

**Mandate**

Improve revenue performance and execution reliability across commercial, product, and operations.

**What they are accountable for**

P&L, prioritisation, leadership alignment, and delivery against a small set of critical outcomes.

**Typical constraints**

Fragmented ownership across revenue lines; competing priorities; limited tolerance for open-ended initiatives.

**What a relevant conversation looks like**

Turning a broad set of initiatives into an executable operating plan tied to measurable revenue outcomes.

### Persona 2: The Chief Revenue / Commercial Leader (Revenue Owner)

**Who**

CRO, Chief Commercial Officer, EVP Revenue, or equivalent.

**Mandate**

Deliver revenue targets across direct, programmatic, partnerships, commerce, and new products.

**What they are accountable for**

Revenue performance, sales execution, pipeline quality, pricing/packaging, and commercial forecasting.

**Typical constraints**

Product promises vs delivery reality; inconsistent measurement; sales capacity and enablement gaps.

**What a relevant conversation looks like**

Improving commercial execution by tightening packaging, handoffs, and measurement without reorganising the entire company.

### Persona 3: The COO / Chief of Staff (Operating Model Owner)

**Who**

COO, Chief of Staff, SVP Strategy & Operations in an operator-facing role.

**Mandate**

Increase execution cadence and reduce cross-functional friction across revenue, product, data, and delivery teams.

**What they are accountable for**

Operating rhythm, prioritisation, governance, and delivery throughput.

**Typical constraints**

Too many parallel initiatives; unclear decision rights; stakeholder misalignment; limited bandwidth.

**What a relevant conversation looks like**

Designing a simple operating model (cadence + decisioning + accountability) that improves speed and reliability.

### Persona 4: The Ads / Monetisation Product Leader (Build + Package Owner)

**Who**

Head/VP Product for Ads, Monetisation, Marketplace, or Retail Media.

**Mandate**

Ship products that can be sold repeatedly and supported operationally.

**What they are accountable for**

Roadmap, packaging, partner requirements, and adoption/retention of monetisation capabilities.

**Typical constraints**

Ambiguous requirements from commercial teams; measurement disputes; dependencies across data/engineering.

**What a relevant conversation looks like**

Making the roadmap commercially legible (what gets built, how it’s sold, how it’s measured) and reducing delivery drag.

### Persona 5: The Value Creation / Operating Partner (Execution Sponsor)

**Who**

PE operating partner, value creation lead, portfolio ops leader (with media/adtech exposure).

**Mandate**

Drive post-investment execution: integration, KPI clarity, operating cadence, and revenue improvement.

**What they are accountable for**

Near-term traction (90–180 days), measurable operational improvements, and de-risking the plan.

**Typical constraints**

Limited internal capacity; leadership change; uneven data quality; pressure for fast results.

**What a relevant conversation looks like**

Rapid diagnostic → prioritised plan → operating cadence that produces measurable progress quickly.
