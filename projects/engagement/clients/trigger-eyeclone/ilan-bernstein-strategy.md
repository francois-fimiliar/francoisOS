## Voice & Tone

**Tone:** [NEEDS INPUT — pull from existing content and content audit]
**Use:** Founder-led framing. Specific pain points (runway, margin, cash visibility, exit readiness). Content that answers a real business problem.
**Avoid:** Service-led framing. Commercial pressure too early. Hard CTAs. Generic thought leadership without substance.

**Working assumption until confirmed:** Direct, operator voice from someone who has lived the finance and M&A process — not a consultant packaging it. Comments should feel like advice from someone in the room, not a pitch from outside it.

**Note:** Ilan runs two businesses (Trigger + Eyeclone) from one personal account. Comments should signal both worlds without confusing positioning. Use intent signals in posts to determine which angle to lead from.

## Comment Types — Rotate These

| Type | When to use | Example opener |
|------|------------|----------------|
| Insight add | Practical M&A or financial visibility angle | "The part founders often miss in this stage is..." |
| Experience bridge | Connect to deal or financial process experience | "Seen this specific problem derail a raise at the 11th hour..." |
| Contrarian | Push back on generic fundraising or exit advice | "This is right for most cases — the exception is when your EBITDA story isn't clean..." |
| Question | Invite dialogue from founders/CFOs | "Curious whether this changes when you're 6 months from an exit vs. just starting to prepare..." |
| Amplify | Strengthen the most practically useful point | "The runway visibility piece especially — that's what investors actually check first." |

**Rules:**
- 2–4 sentences minimum. No one-liners.
- Batch to 8–10am local time — early comments on fresh posts get more algorithm visibility.
- Lead with the problem, not the service.
- Use intent signals to determine Trigger vs. Eyeclone angle: funding/exit language = Trigger, cash/margin/forecasting language = Eyeclone.
- Never pitch either product in a comment.
- Sound like an operator who has done it, not an advisor who recommends it.

## Prospect Targeting

**Trigger ICP:**
- P1 Funding Journey: Founders/CEOs and CFOs at companies 6–18 months from a raise (headcount 11–2,000, revenue $5M–$200M+)
- P1 Exit Readiness: CEOs/Founders, CFOs at companies 6–24 months from exit (industries: Healthcare, Finance, Accounting, Real Estate)
- Advisory & Transaction Firms: Partners/MDs at finance/M&A/CPA advisory firms (headcount 11–500)

**Eyeclone ICP:**
- P2 Exec Teams: Founders/CEOs and CFOs at growth-stage companies with real-time financial visibility gaps (headcount 11–1,000, revenue $1M–$100M+)

**Geography:** US primary, UK secondary
**How to identify posts to comment on:**
- Trigger signals: "fundraising," "runway," "exit," "M&A," "term sheet," "due diligence," "Series A/B/C," "acquisition"
- Eyeclone signals: "cash flow," "QuickBooks," "Xero," "forecasting," "financial visibility," "scenario planning," "profit margin"
- People in connect/warm queue — prioritise first

**Avoid:**
- Pure investment/VC content with no founder angle
- Corporate finance for public companies (different buyer)
- Generic business advice with no finance signal
