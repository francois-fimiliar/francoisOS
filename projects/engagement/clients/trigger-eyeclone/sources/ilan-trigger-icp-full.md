# Trigger – Consolidated Ideal Customer Profiles (ICP)

*Source: Trigger Ideal Customer Profile 1 (Jan26)*

## Trigger's Core Purpose

Trigger exists because strategy, software, and headcount alone do not create enterprise value — execution does.

As businesses grow, finance and operations become more complex, decisions carry higher stakes, and the cost of poor execution compounds quickly. Trigger closes the gap between insight and outcome by executing where value is actually created — across finance and operations, enabled by the right people and technology.

**How Trigger creates value:**
- Owning execution, not just advising
- Embedding accountable teams into real operating environments
- Aligning finance and operations around measurable outcomes
- Using technology as an enabler when it accelerates speed, lowers cost, or improves control

## ICP 1: Growth-Stage Companies

**Summary:** Growth-stage companies intentionally preparing for their next major inflection point — scaling efficiently, raising capital, or exiting — requiring enterprise-grade financial leadership and execution.

### Three Growth-Stage Journeys

**1. Exit Readiness Journey (6–24 months)**
- Characteristics: Founder-led or investor-backed, clear intent to sell/recap/merge, increasing interaction with bankers/buyers/PE
- Primary needs: Buyer-aligned financial models and KPIs, valuation and scenario clarity, diligence-ready data room
- Why Trigger: Helps shape the financial story buyers pay for — aligning performance, metrics, and governance to valuation drivers early

**2. Funding Journey (6–18 months)**
- Characteristics: Founder-led or growth-stage, preparing for institutional investors or lenders, sensitive to dilution/terms/timing
- Primary needs: Investor- and lender-ready financial models, capital KPIs (CAC/LTV, runway, DSCR, leverage), clear funding scenarios
- Why Trigger: Prepares companies to raise capital with clarity and leverage

**3. Scalable Growth Journey**
- Characteristics: Not actively raising or exiting yet, growth outpacing financial structure, leadership feeling reactive
- Primary needs: Scalable FP&A and forecasting, performance visibility and scenario planning, a finance rhythm that supports growth
- Why Trigger: Institutionalizes finance early — embedding structure, insight, and discipline required to scale without chaos or margin erosion

### Firmographics
- $5M–$200M+ revenue
- Growth-stage, increasing operational and financial complexity
- Common in Healthcare, Finance, Accounting, and Real Estate (not exclusive)
- On an active Exit Readiness, Funding, or Scalable Growth journey

### Buyer Personas
- Founder / CEO
- CFO / VP Finance
- COO
- PE Operating Partner

### Core Problems
- Financial complexity outpacing internal capability
- Lack of forward-looking insight
- High-stakes decisions made with incomplete data
- Execution gaps between strategy and outcomes

### Buying Triggers
- Imminent exit or capital raise
- Board or investor pressure
- Rapid growth creating operational strain
- Desire to professionalize finance ahead of inflection

### Success Criteria
- Clear, credible financial narrative
- Decision-grade models and KPIs
- Reduced risk and surprises
- Confidence at the board, investor, or buyer level

## ICP 2: Finance, M&A & CPA Advisory Firms

**Summary:** Advice-led finance, M&A, and CPA advisory firms that sell high-stakes outcomes to growth-stage companies and require a trusted execution partner to deliver defined components of those outcomes under their brand.

### Firmographics
- Finance, M&A, and CPA advisory firms (not end clients)
- Partner-led or founder-led professional services firms
- Advisory-led revenue model (retainers, projects, success-based fees)
- Serving growth-stage clients across multiple industries
- Experiencing delivery strain as client demand scales

### Buyer Personas
- Founder / Managing Partner
- Advisory Practice Leader (Finance, M&A, CFO, Transaction Services)
- Delivery, Operations, or Resource Lead

### Core Problems
- Execution capacity limits deal flow and growth
- Internal teams are expensive and hard to scale
- Hiring ahead of demand increases risk
- Delivery strain threatens credibility and margins

### Buying Triggers
- Increase in client demand or transaction activity
- New advisory offerings without delivery infrastructure
- Margin pressure from internal staffing
- Desire to scale without diluting partner oversight

### What Trigger Executes (under the advisory firm's brand)
- **Exit Readiness Execution:** buyer-aligned FP&A, valuation modeling, KPI infrastructure, diligence readiness
- **Funding & Capital Raise Execution:** capital-grade financial models, investor/lender KPIs, scenario planning, data-room readiness
- **Scalable Growth & Finance Transformation:** FP&A, forecasting, reporting, and finance rhythm execution

### Success Criteria
- Ability to sell and deliver larger engagements
- Protected or improved advisory margins
- High client satisfaction and retention
- Flexible scaling without fixed headcount

## ICP 3: CPA Audit Firms

**Summary:** CPA audit firms facing capacity constraints and cost pressure during peak audit cycles that need reliable execution support to deliver audits on time and to standard.

### Firmographics
- Regional and national CPA audit firms
- Audit-heavy practices
- Performing HUD, EBP, Not-for-Profit, For-Profit audits, and Healthcare Cost Reports
- Recurring seasonal demand spikes
- Serving regulated or complex entities

### Buyer Personas
- Audit Partner
- Managing Partner
- Audit Practice Leader
- Resource / Staffing Lead

### Core Problems
- Audit capacity shortages
- Difficulty hiring and retaining experienced auditors
- Margin pressure during busy season
- Risk of missed deadlines or quality issues

### Buying Triggers
- Busy season demand spikes
- New audit wins without staff to deliver
- Regulatory or complexity-driven audit requirements
- Attrition within audit teams

### Success Criteria
- Audits delivered on time and to standard
- Reduced staff burnout
- Protected audit margins
- Predictable seasonal scaling

## Shared Buying Psychology

Trigger wins when prospects believe:
- Execution is where value is created or destroyed
- Fixed headcount creates risk
- Outcomes matter more than hours
- Ownership and accountability cannot be outsourced blindly

---

## Non-ICP Profiles (Who Trigger Should Avoid)

- Compliance-only CPA firms (price-led, billable-hour model)
- Very small or lifestyle practices (<5–10 employees)
- Firms selling time, not outcomes (strictly hourly billing)
- Firms seeking pure staff augmentation (bodies, not ownership)
- Enterprise firms with inflexible procurement
- Early-stage startups without budget or urgency (<$1M revenue)
- Low-ownership or low-accountability cultures
