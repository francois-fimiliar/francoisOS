# Trigger – One-Page ICP Summaries

*Source: Trigger – One-Page ICP Summaries (Jan26)*
*Purpose: Executive- and sales-ready summaries for quick alignment, qualification, and messaging*

---

## ICP 1: Growth-Stage Companies

**Who They Are**
Growth-stage companies ($5M–$200M+ revenue) facing increasing financial and operational complexity as they scale, raise capital, or prepare for an exit. Common in Healthcare, Finance, Accounting, and Real Estate.

**Why They Buy Trigger**
They need immediate, experienced execution teams to make a real impact — without adding permanent headcount. Under pressure to improve EBITDA and cash control, make high-stakes decisions with confidence, and professionalize finance and operations ahead of an inflection point.

**When They Buy**
- Preparing for an exit (6–24 months)
- Planning a capital raise (6–18 months)
- Growth outpacing financial visibility and control
- Board or investor pressure to level up execution

**What Trigger Delivers**
- End-to-end execution across finance and operations
- Embedded teams that own outcomes
- Custom automation and infrastructure when needed
- Measurable improvements in visibility, control, and value creation

**What Success Looks Like**
- Decision-grade financials and KPIs
- Reduced execution risk and surprises
- Confidence at the board, investor, or buyer level
- Clear linkage between execution and enterprise value

---

## ICP 2: Finance, M&A & CPA Advisory Firms

**Who They Are**
Advice-led finance, M&A, and CPA advisory firms serving growth-stage clients and selling high-stakes outcomes. Typically partner- or founder-led firms with strong client relationships and growing delivery demand.

**Why They Buy Trigger**
They need scalable execution capacity to deliver client outcomes — without increasing fixed cost, delivery risk, or internal complexity. Want to protect margins, scale delivery without hiring ahead of demand, and maintain credibility with clients.

**When They Buy**
- Growing deal flow or transaction volume
- New service offerings without delivery infrastructure
- Internal team strain or margin pressure
- Desire to move from advice to outcomes

**What Trigger Delivers**
- White-labeled execution under the advisory firm's brand
- Selective execution within client journeys (exit readiness, funding, scalable growth)
- Embedded teams that integrate seamlessly with advisory leadership

**What Success Looks Like**
- Ability to sell and deliver larger engagements
- Improved client satisfaction and retention
- Preserved or improved margins
- Flexible scaling without permanent headcount

---

## ICP 3: CPA Audit Firms

**Who They Are**
Regional and national CPA audit firms with audit-heavy practices and recurring seasonal demand spikes. Performing HUD, EBP, Not-for-Profit, For-Profit audits, and Healthcare Cost Reports.

**Why They Buy Trigger**
They need reliable audit execution capacity to meet demand, maintain quality, and protect margins — without permanent hiring.

**When They Buy**
- Busy season demand spikes
- New audit wins without staffing capacity
- Auditor attrition
- Increased regulatory or reporting complexity

**What Trigger Delivers**
- Experienced audit professionals
- On-demand execution and capacity augmentation
- Seamless integration with existing audit teams

**What Success Looks Like**
- Audits delivered on time and to standard
- Reduced staff burnout
- Predictable seasonal scaling
- Protected audit margins
