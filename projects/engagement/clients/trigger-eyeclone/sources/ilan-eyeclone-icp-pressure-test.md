# Eyeclone – Ideal Customer Profile & Executive Pressure Test

*Source: Eyeclone – Ideal Customer Profile & Executive Pressure Test Jan26*
*Status: FINAL / LOCKED*
*Purpose: Diagnose fit, urgency, and executive readiness for Eyeclone*

---

## ICP Summary

Growth-stage companies where leadership is making high-stakes decisions without real-time visibility into valuation, drivers, and financial cause-and-effect — and cannot afford to fly blind.

## Target Company Profile (Firmographics)

- Revenue: $1M–$100M+ annually
- Stage: Growth-stage with increasing complexity
- Characteristics:
  - Financial decisions materially impact cash, risk, and enterprise value
  - Reporting exists, but confidence does not
  - Insight lags behind decision speed

## Buyer Personas

**Founder / CEO**
- Owns growth, risk, and enterprise value
- Makes frequent high-stakes decisions
- Feels the weight of uncertainty, even when growth is strong

**CFO / VP Finance / Fractional CFO**
- Accountable for financial clarity and credibility
- Buried in explanation, reconciliation, and scenario work
- Acts as the bottleneck between data and decisions

**Influencers:** COO, Financial Controller, Board Members / Investors

## Core Problems (From the Customer's Reality)

These leaders are not incompetent — they are capacity constrained. They experience:

- Episodic confidence instead of continuous confidence
- Delayed understanding of why numbers move
- Decisions made before full impact is visible
- Scenario modeling that is slow or manual
- High cognitive load from fragmented data and tools
- Anxiety around data accuracy, completeness, and execution follow-through

**Net Result:** Leaders operate with partial visibility and fragile confidence as stakes rise.

## Buying Triggers (Why Now)

- Growth increasing financial and operational complexity
- Capital raises, debt, or exit preparation (6–24 months)
- Board or investor pressure for stronger insight
- Margin compression or cash surprises
- Founder or executive fatigue from constant second-guessing

## Success Criteria (What Winning Looks Like)

- Continuous confidence in financial understanding
- Real-time visibility into performance, cash, and valuation
- Clear explanations for cause and effect
- Ability to see decision and scenario impact before acting
- Fewer surprises and less reactive firefighting
- Stronger executive, board, and investor confidence

## ICP Pressure Test: Executive Reality Check

*These questions diagnose fit, not sell. If they create hesitation, discomfort, or qualified answers — the prospect is likely a strong fit.*

| Pressure Area | Question | Reality Check |
|---|---|---|
| Confidence & Comfort | Do you genuinely feel confident in your financial understanding during this growth phase — every day? | Comfort is episodic. Confidence is fragile. |
| Real-Time Visibility | Do you know your numbers in real time — and understand why they change without asking for explanations? | Data exists, but understanding lags. |
| Decision Impact | Can you make major decisions and clearly see their financial and valuation impact before acting? | Decisions are fast; impact is understood later. |
| Scenario Modeling | Can you model pricing, hiring, spend, or growth scenarios in real time without building new spreadsheets or waiting on analysis? | Scenario planning is possible, but slow and effort-heavy. |
| Time & Cognitive Load | Do you have time every day to proactively think about what could go wrong and what is going well? | Leaders react more than they anticipate. |
| Execution Feedback | Can you confirm whether actions were executed — and whether they worked — in real time? | Feedback loops are delayed and fragmented. |
| Information Overload | Can you process and synthesize large volumes of company information across systems in real time? | Human capacity is exceeded. |
| Data Trust & Security | Are you confident your data is clean, complete, and working correctly at all times — and fully secure? | Trust is assumed, not continuously verified. |

## What These Answers Reveal

These leaders are capable — but overloaded. They lack:
- Time
- Continuous intelligence
- Explainability
- Confidence loops between action and outcome

As complexity grows, confidence becomes episodic instead of continuous.

## Non-ICP Profiles (Who This Is NOT For)

- Very early-stage startups (<$1M revenue)
- Compliance-only or price-driven organizations
- Businesses satisfied with static or backward-looking reports
- Teams unwilling to act on insight
- Organizations seeking AI to replace accountability
