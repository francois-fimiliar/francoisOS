# Outreach Strategy — Rob Bate

*Company: FrontlineXP*
*Last updated: 2026-04-29*

---

## Philosophy

Rob's DMs are the moment a comment-section presence becomes a direct conversation. Every DM should feel like it comes from an operationally credible practitioner who noticed something specific — not a sales sequence that fired on a signal.

**Warm outreach only. No exceptions.**

---

## Intent Threshold

Minimum: 2-3 engagement touches from Rob + one awareness signal from the prospect.

| Signal | Intent | Action |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Replied to Rob's comment | Very high | DM within 48hrs |
| Commented on Rob's post | Very high | DM within 48hrs |
| Sent unprompted connection request | High | DM within 48hrs |
| Viewed Rob's profile after interaction | High | DM within 72hrs |
| Liked 2+ Rob posts same week | Medium | Wait for next signal |
| Appeared in same comment section 2+ times | Medium | Wait for next signal |

---

## Message Framework

### Message 1

**Reference signals to use:**
- Their reply to Rob's comment — reference the specific point they made
- Their post about a frontline workforce challenge Rob commented on
- Their profile view — if they viewed after Rob commented on their content

**Topics Rob can credibly open on:**
- Frontline workforce transformation and what's actually hard about it
- WFM platform decisions (UKG, Legion, Dayforce, Quinyx — genuine no-bias perspective)
- Labour optimisation in retail/hospitality at enterprise scale
- Employee experience for frontline/deskless workers
- Store operations and multi-site management challenges

**Example opener structure:**
> "Saw your [reply/comment] on [X's post] about [specific topic] — the point about [their specific observation] is something we see constantly at the [enterprise retail/hospitality] scale.
> [One observation that extends their point.]
> [One question — e.g. 'How are you approaching [specific challenge] at [company type]?']"

**Rules:**
- Under 60 words / under 300 characters
- Reference the specific thing they said — not the topic in general
- No FrontlineXP mention in Message 1
- No pitch, no CTA, no calendar link
- Sound like a practitioner, not a consultant selling

---

### Message 2 (if no reply, 5-7 days later)

Use a new angle — ideally triggered by something they posted or shared after Message 1.

> "[Reference something new they posted or a new observation.]
> [Add a thought or a resource that's genuinely relevant — no pitch.]
> [Different question from Message 1.]"

**Hard limit: 2 DMs without a reply. Then return to comment mode for 3-4 weeks.**

---

## Voice Rules

- Practitioner tone — not polished, not consultant-speak
- Specific and operational — "at Sainsbury's/Boots scale" not "large enterprise"
- Zero vendor bias in any reference to WFM platforms
- Never name FrontlineXP until the conversation has opened naturally

**Avoid:**
- "Hope this finds you well"
- "I noticed your company..."
- "Would love to jump on a quick call"
- Generic WFM commentary that any vendor could have written

---

## No-Go List

- Cold DMs — no signal, no DM
- Pitching FrontlineXP in Messages 1 or 2
- Calendar link before a conversation exists
- DMing the same day as first connecting
- More than 2 DMs without a reply
- Healthcare sector contacts (excluded ICP)

---

## Strongest Prospect Signals for Rob

Given Rob's ICP (enterprise retail/hospitality ops leaders), the highest-value signals are:
- COOs/Operations Directors at perfect-fit companies (Halfords, H&B, Morrisons, Greene King level) replying to his comments
- HR/People leaders at enterprise retail posting about workforce challenges
- Anyone at a company running a WFM platform Rob knows well, expressing frustration or evaluation intent
