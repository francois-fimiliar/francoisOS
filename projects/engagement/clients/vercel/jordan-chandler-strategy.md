## Voice & Tone

**Tone:** Professionally credible, technically literate, not sales-y. Thought leader, not sales rep.
**Use:** Technical depth, institutional knowledge of developer tooling, regional specificity for UKI and GCC markets.
**Avoid:** Sales-focused framing. Generic enterprise sales content. Commercial pressure. Content that reads like a pitch. Anything that makes Jordan sound like a rep, not a practitioner.

---

## Comment Types — Rotate These

| Type | When to use | Example opener |
|------|------------|----------------|
| Insight add | Technical perspective that extends the post | "The edge runtime piece especially — what most teams underestimate is..." |
| Experience bridge | Connect to regional or enterprise-scale context | "Seeing this play out differently in the GCC market..." |
| Contrarian | Push back on a generalisation with technical specificity | "This holds for smaller teams, but at enterprise scale the constraint is usually..." |
| Question | Invite dialogue from CTOs/VPs Engineering | "Curious how this decision changes when you're standardising across 50+ engineering teams..." |
| Amplify | Strengthen the most technically interesting point | "The DX piece here is the real unlock — that's what actually drives adoption at the team level." |

**Rules:**
- 2–4 sentences minimum. No one-liners.
- Batch to 8–10am local time — early comments on fresh posts get more algorithm visibility.
- Every comment must lead with technical insight or regional specificity — never a product reference.
- UKI and GCC context is an underused differentiator — use it.
- Sound like an engineering leader, not an AE.
- Never pitch Vercel in a comment.

## Prospect Targeting

**ICP:** CTOs, VP Engineering, engineering leaders, technical decision-makers
**Industries:** SaaS, software, tech — companies with significant frontend/web infrastructure needs
**Geography:** UK & Ireland + Gulf Cooperation Council (UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Kuwait, Bahrain, Oman)
**Stage:** Enterprise and mid-market

**How to identify posts to comment on:**
- Posts using language: "frontend," "developer experience," "infrastructure," "Next.js," "edge computing," "web performance," "deployment," "developer tools"
- People in connect/warm queue — prioritise first
- Engineering leadership discussing build-vs-buy decisions, DX investment, or frontend architecture
- GCC market posts: UAE/Saudi tech investment, digital transformation, Vision 2030 (Saudi), developer ecosystem growth

**Avoid:**
- Pure backend / data engineering content (outside Jordan's lane)
- HR, finance, or ops content unrelated to engineering leadership
- Generic business/leadership content with no technical angle
