# Outreach Strategy — Valentin Kalis (Val)

*Company: Inference Chain*
*Last updated: 2026-04-29*

---

## Philosophy

Val's DMs come from someone who has fixed things that were broken. The "fixer" framing is his strongest asset — if a prospect is in pain (slow implementation, bad data, broken RELEX relationship), Val is the person who's lived that problem and solved it. DMs should feel like a practitioner reaching out because they noticed something specific, not a consultant prospecting.

**Warm outreach only. No exceptions.**

## Message Framework

### Message 1

**Voice:** Casual, direct, confident, occasionally self-deprecating. Writes like he talks. First-person, story-led. Not polished.

**Topics Val can credibly open on:**
- RELEX implementation — what actually takes the longest and why
- Clean data as the precondition for any successful supply chain system
- Blue Yonder to RELEX migrations — the real complexity
- Fast implementation — how the Adidas timeline happened
- Demand planning and S&OP at enterprise grocery/FMCG/manufacturing scale
- What "fixer" situations actually look like from the inside

**Example opener structure:**
> "[Reference exactly what they said — their specific frustration, question, or observation.]
> [One honest, specific reaction — Val's operator experience, not polished advice.]
> [One question — direct, genuinely curious, no leading toward a pitch.]"

**Hard numbers can appear naturally:** "We cut the Adidas go-live to 10 weeks from 16 — the data piece was the only unlock." Works as context, not a brag.

**Rules:**
- Under 60 words / under 300 characters
- Sound like Val talking, not a consultant writing
- No Inference Chain mention in Message 1
- No competitor names directly
- No pitch, no CTA, no calendar

---

### Message 2 (if no reply, 5-7 days later)

Different angle. New signal if possible. Still conversational, not chasing.

**Hard limit: 2 DMs without a reply.**

---

## Voice Rules

**Use:** First-person, casual, specific hard numbers where natural, fixer framing
**Avoid:** Corporate jargon, polished language, competitor names, politics, religion, content that positions Inference Chain as a pure delivery shop

---

## No-Go List

- Cold DMs
- Competitor names in any message
- Pitch or Inference Chain mention in Messages 1 or 2
- Calendar link before conversation opens
- More than 2 DMs without a reply
- Over-polished language (kills Val's authentic voice)

---

## Strongest Prospect Signals for Val

- Supply Chain Directors/VPs at enterprise grocery, FMCG, retail, or manufacturing posting about implementation pain
- Anyone posting about Blue Yonder frustrations or evaluating alternatives
- RELEX AEs engaging with Val's content (partner network visibility)
- Prospects at Adidas/Aldi-tier companies expressing supply chain transformation challenges
