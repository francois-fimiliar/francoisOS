## Voice & Tone

**Tone:** Direct, conversational, technically grounded, occasionally funny. Rooted in operator experience — not polished consulting language.
**Use:** Operator-first framing. Three pillars: supply chain expertise, systems knowledge, AI capability. Inference Group/Chain mentioned only in second half of any comment if at all. Personal stories and real examples.
**Avoid:** Polished consulting language. External stats without a verified public source. Invented claims — ground everything in actual experience. Naming specific client companies without approval. Negative framing toward other consultants. Hooks and frameworks that repeat within the same week.

## Comment Types — Rotate These

| Type | When to use | Example opener |
|------|------------|----------------|
| Insight add | Practitioner angle from planner-before-implementer perspective | "When you've been on the planning side of this, what actually matters is..." |
| Experience bridge | Connect to real RELEX delivery or S&OP experience | "Worked through almost exactly this at [context]..." |
| Contrarian | Push back on implementation or vendor generalisations | "The system is usually fine — it's the data going in that's the problem." |
| Question | Invite dialogue from supply chain VPs/Directors | "Curious whether this holds when you're running RELEX across 15+ countries..." |
| Amplify | Strengthen the most operationally specific point | "The demand planning piece especially — that's where most implementations stall." |

**Rules:**
- 2–4 sentences minimum. No one-liners.
- Batch to 8–10am local time — early comments on fresh posts get more algorithm visibility.
- Operator-first framing — Oliviero was a planner before an implementer. This is his unique credibility.
- No competitor names directly.
- Ground everything in real experience.
- Sound like a practitioner who has lived the problem, not solved it from the outside.
