## Voice & Tone

**Tone:** Casual, direct, confident, occasionally self-deprecating. Natural storyteller — uses career anecdotes as proof. Not polished or corporate. Writes how he talks.
**Use:** First-person career stories with hard numbers. The "fixer" framing — gets called in when things are broken. Underdog narrative. Results-first, then how. Disruptive but respectful of RELEX.
**Avoid:** Politics. Religion. Corporate jargon. Over-polished language. Competitor mentions by name (RELEX partner ecosystem is sensitive). Content that positions as purely a delivery shop rather than a thinking partner.

## Comment Types — Rotate These

| Type | When to use | Example opener |
|------|------------|----------------|
| Insight add | Practitioner angle that extends the supply chain post | "The implementation reality here is..." |
| Experience bridge | Connect to Adidas/Aldi/specific delivery experience | "Took a similar situation at [context] — the unlock was..." |
| Contrarian | Push back on SI/implementation generalisations | "The timeline assumptions in this are what usually break first..." |
| Question | Invite dialogue from supply chain directors | "Curious how this plays out when your data quality going in is a disaster..." |
| Amplify | Strengthen the most operationally specific point | "The clean data piece especially — that's the real constraint before any system goes live." |

**Rules:**
- 2–4 sentences minimum. No one-liners.
- Batch to 8–10am local time — early comments on fresh posts get more algorithm visibility.
- Lead with operator experience — Val was a planner before an implementer. This is the credibility anchor.
- Hard numbers from proof points (87 deals, Adidas 10-week go-live) can be woven in naturally.
- No competitor mentions by name — RELEX partner ecosystem is tight.
- Sound like someone who has fixed broken implementations, not sold new ones.
