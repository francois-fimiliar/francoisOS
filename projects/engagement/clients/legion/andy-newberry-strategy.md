## Comment Types — Rotate These

| Type | When to use | Example opener |
|------|------------|----------------|
| Insight add | Retail/hospitality-specific operational angle | "The scheduling piece especially — in a national retail estate, the margin for error is..." |
| Experience bridge | Connect to multi-site retail or hospitality operational reality | "Seen this pattern in retailers/operators with 100+ sites — the seasonal demand problem compounds fast." |
| Contrarian | Push back on generic workforce/HR takes | "The retention framing here is right, but the lever most operators pull last is schedule predictability..." |
| Question | Invite dialogue from Ops/HR/Finance leaders | "Curious how this decision changes when you're managing 20,000 frontline colleagues across 500 stores..." |
| Amplify | Strengthen the most operationally specific point | "The labour waste piece is real — but the retention cost from unpredictable hours is the one that doesn't get measured." |

**Rules:**
- 2–4 sentences minimum. No one-liners.
- Batch to 8–10am local time — early comments on fresh posts get more algorithm visibility.
- Retail and hospitality specificity always — not generic WFM commentary.
- Multi-stakeholder framing — comments should resonate with Ops, Finance, AND HR.
- Never pitch Legion directly.
- Andy's ICP = retail and hospitality. Never drift into FM (Rob Rooney's territory).

## Prospect Targeting

**ICP:** COO, Operations Director, CFO, Head of Finance, CHRO, HR Director, Head of Retail Ops, Head of Store Operations

**Geography:** EMEA (decision-makers at HQ or remote)

**How to identify posts to comment on:**
- Posts about: store operations, field operations, labour productivity, rota, scheduling, seasonal recruitment, frontline workforce, flexible working
- Hospitality-specific: front of house, back of house, shift patterns, guest experience, occupancy, RevPAR
- People in connect/warm queue — prioritise first
- Perfect-fit companies posting: M&S, H&M, Greggs, NEXT, Card Factory, Whitbread, SSP Group, Mitchells & Butlers, Holland & Barrett, Tesco

**Avoid:**
- FM content (Rob Rooney's territory — don't cross over)
- Recruitment/talent acquisition content with no operations angle
- Companies outside Andy's enterprise scale range
